## 🚄 The Train-Friendly 14-Day Itinerary

### Days 1–6: The Shinkansen High-Speed Loop


* Days 1 & 2: [Fukuoka](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0gqkd)
* Arrive at [Hakata Station](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/023_vc). Celebrate with Hakata ramen at the riverside yatai stalls.
   * Take a local 30-minute train to [Dazaifu Tenmangu Shrine](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/07v_03) on Day 2 to stroll under early spring plum and cherry blossoms.
* Days 3 & 4: [Nagasaki](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/05gqf)
* Board the Relay Kamome express, transferring seamlessly to the sleek West Kyushu Shinkansen to reach [Nagasaki](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/05gqf) in about 1.5 hours.
   * Explore the [Peace Park](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0bx1j8) and historical Dejima by riding the city's charming vintage streetcars.
* Days 5 & 6: [Kumamoto](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0gp6_0) & [Kagoshima](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/049wm)
* Zip from [Nagasaki](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/05gqf) back through [Hakata](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/023_vc) down to [Kumamoto](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0gp6_0) on the Kyushu Shinkansen (under 2 hours). Walk from the station to [Kumamoto Castle](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/01x1g5) to see the sakura.
   * On Day 6, hop back on the Shinkansen for a brief 45-minute ride to Kagoshima-Chuo. Take the city view bus to see the active volcano Sakurajima steaming across the bay.


### Days 7–10: The Scenic Trans-Kyushu Rail Line


* Days 7 & 8: [Mount Aso](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/03t72m)
* Catch the limited express Aso Boy! or the regular JR Hohi Main Line train cutting directly east across the island's volcanic caldera to [Aso Station](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/04ycl73).
   * From [Aso Station](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/04ycl73), a reliable local transit bus takes you straight up to the Aso Volcano Crater rim.
* Days 9 & 10: [Beppu Onsen](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0hgl1fl)
* Continue east on the JR Hohi Line directly into [Beppu](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/01wqyp).
   * Spend two full days utilizing local buses to visit the steaming "Hells" and treating yourself to hot sand baths.


### Days 11–14: Shikoku Rail & Art Islands


* Days 11 & 12: [Matsuyama](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0gp6sg)
* Walk or take a short taxi to the Beppu Ferry Port. Board the Uwajima運輸 Ferry to cross the sea to [Yawatahama Port](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/120jr2_y) on Shikoku (approx. 3 hours).
   * Hop onto a direct JR Shikoku Limited Express train to [Matsuyama](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0gp6sg). Use the city's historic trams to visit the wooden [Matsuyama Castle](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/08d5t3) and soak in [Dogo Onsen](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/1237q26n).
* Days 13 & 14: [Takamatsu](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0gp6rn) & Naoshima
* Ride the JR Yosan Line Limited Express (Ishizuchi) along the northern coast of Shikoku straight into [Takamatsu](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0gp6rn) (2.5 hours).
   * Spend the afternoon wandering through the magnificent [Ritsurin Garden](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/06_vz1).
   * On Day 14, walk out of Takamatsu Station directly to the ferry pier for a 50-minute boat ride to Naoshima Art Island to view Yayoi Kusama's famous pumpkin before heading out.
